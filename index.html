A Decade of Hiking (Mis)Adventures

Welcome!

My husband always jokes that my hiking and camping plans lead to trouble. So, I decided to document my most memorable misadventures. Enjoy the highlights!

Garden of the Gods, Illinois

October 2021

My husband and I camped overnight & did the short loop around these beautiful sandstone rock formations. All went well until we came back to an almost flat tire. Hard to avoid that one and thankfully we were able to get home safely. 10/10 would do again.

Mount Sherman, Colorado

August 2018

While spending a summer out in Colorado, my husband came to visit. I had done a few other 14ers at that point and wanted to try a relatively short one with him. We decided to approach Mt. Sherman from its western trailhead. We arrived to a crowded trailhead with cars parked all along the forest road. We started hiking from the road, and the trail was well marked up to an old mining shack. However, past this mining shack the trail turned into a very steep uphill path of scree. My husband wanted to turn back since his shoes didn't have great grip. I tried to reassure him that the trail would probably get better past the next hill top of scree. Good thing he didn't listen to me! As we were discussing, a pair of hikers descended towards us with helmets on and mountaineering gear. Turns out we were on the wrong trail. By the time we turned around and were back at the forest and some of the cars had left, we saw where we missed the correct trailhead. Unfortunately we did not have enough time to try again that day. I was able to complete the trail with friends later that month from the eastern trailhead and laughed when I was on the summit looking down to the west and saw how difficult our "trail" would have been to get to the summit. Whoops!

Chicot State Park, Louisiana

March 2017

On my way from New Orleans to Houston to visit friends, I needed a place to stay overnight. Since I was in grad school at the time and traveling alone, the cheaper the better. I found a state park with backcountry campsites a couple of miles from the parking lot by a beautiful lake. Seemed easy enough for a quick overnight camp. I arrived late in the day and hiked through the forest along lakeshore to a very small peninsula where I set up my tent. The park was silent and eerily beautiful with its black water, big cypress trees, and spanish moss...that is until I saw a gator about 10 feet away in the water. At this point it was dusk, so I didn't want to risk walking back as the sky was getting dark. I watched the gator, and it eventually went on its way. Needless to say I didn't get much sleep that night. But, still worth it to see the beautiful sunrise over the lake.

North Cascades National Park, Washington

July 2016

On another road trip from visiting friends in Vancouver, BC back to my then home in Portland, OR, I decided to take a detour to visit North Cascades National Park. I had wanted to see the beautiful park for some time and knew this would be my last chance for a while since I was moving to St. Louis later that month. Knowing that the hikes might not be very accessible yet due to snow still present in July, I was able to find one trailhead that was snow free. I alloted myself a few hours of hiking before I needed to start driving the 6 hours back to Portland. I hiked for about an hour and a half admiring the stunning snow capped mountain peaks before turning back. Shortly into the second half of my hike I slipped coming off a wet patch of scree and fell twisting my ankle. Thankfully I was able to hobble back to the car and drive back safely. However, by the time I reached Portland, my ankle had swelled up to the size of a golf ball. But, would I do it again? Yes, 100% for those views.

Three Fingered Jack, Oregon

August 2014

One of my last hikes I completed while living in Central Oregon was Canyon Creek Meadows in the Mount Jefferson Wilderness. A relatively short, easy, and popular day hike, I felt confident doing the loop trail alone. However, as I was heading back and walking through an area of burned forest, I realized there were multiple trails forking from where I had stopped to pick some blueberries. I thought I was on the right trail and followed that for a mile until it came to a lake. I quickly realized there had been no lake mentioned in the trail description I had read before starting the hike. The shadows were getting longer and the burned forest was disorienting, but thankfully I ran/walked back to the junction and was able to find my way safely back to the trailhead before dark. Despite the temporary confusion, all in all a beautiful hike.
